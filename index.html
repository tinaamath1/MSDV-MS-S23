The Colorado River Basin

The Colorado River plays a vital role in the agriculture industry- 80% of the river is used for agriculture, with larger water use seen in southern areas like California and Arizona as the climate allows for crops like broccoli, alfalfa & citrus fruits to grow.

In 1922, as a way to preserve water in the region, a landmark agreement, called The Colorado River Compact was made, dividing up water for each state and Mexico. That policy is still used today.

Imperial County is the largest agricultural water user by county.

According to the Bureau of Reclamation, in 2023, the Imperial Irrigation Distract is projected to use 64% of Californias river allotment, accounting for water requirements for each crop.
